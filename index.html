In-Tray Tracker

How To Use In-Tray Tracker

What This App Tracks

In-Tray Tracker helps you keep recurring inboxes, folders, paper piles, and other information sources from going stale. Each in-tray has its own clearing cadence.

Add An In-Tray

  • Tap Add.
  • Enter the exact name you want shown.
  • Choose how often it should be fully cleared.
  • Add optional notes, then tap Save In-Tray.

Use Your List

  • Tap a card to expand it and see details.
  • Tap the expanded card header to collapse it.
  • Swipe left to mark the in-tray Fully Cleared.
  • Swipe right to mark it Worked On.
  • Use the expanded card buttons if you prefer tapping instead of swiping.

Fully Cleared Vs. Worked On

  • Fully Cleared means the in-tray was completely processed. This resets its cadence timer.
  • Worked On means you made progress, but did not fully clear it. This updates the last worked-on time without resetting the fully cleared cadence.

Status Meaning

  • Good: fully cleared within its cadence.
  • Needs Attention: past its normal cadence, but not severely overdue.
  • Overdue: never fully cleared or more than twice past its cadence.

Edit Or Delete

Tap a card to expand it, then tap Edit. From the edit form, you can save changes or delete that in-tray.

Search, Filter, And Stats

  • Use Search to find in-trays by name or notes.
  • Use the status dropdown to show all, good, needs-attention, or overdue items.
  • Tap the stats bar to toggle between counts and percentages.

Tags

Tags are optional labels for context, such as Home, Work, Church, or a specific device. Use one clear tag per in-tray so filtering stays useful. In-trays without a custom tag are treated as Untagged.

Undo And Backups

  • Undo reverses your last major action.
  • The app reminds you to export once a week after Sunday at 6:00 AM if you have not backed up since then.
  • Export Backup downloads your current list as a JSON file.
  • Import Backup restores a JSON backup and replaces your current list.
